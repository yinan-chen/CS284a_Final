CS284a: Computer Graphics, Spring 2021

Final Project - Water Ripple Simulation

Lloyd Brown, Yinan Chen, Sophie Wu, Weiyan Zhu


Problem Description

Water simulations are challenging due to water’s dynamic nature and the complicated lighting effects that result from its dynamism. Solving physical models precludes efficient real-time simulation. As a result approximate methods arose to improve simulation efficiency. Process-based approximate methods fail to produce complex models given their lack of a physical basis.

Our interactive water ripple simulation is based in a mass-spring model. The surface of the water is represented by a mesh of mass-springs. Ripple effects are created by considering the relationship between points on the water’s surface using Hooke’s law.


Goals and Deliverables

Our deliverable would be implemented using WebGL and Three.js. We are aiming to deliver a final result that would mimic Evan Wallace’s demo of WebGL water. Our MVP would be a water ripple simulation such that users could interact freely with the water surface to make ripples, with raytraced light effects. Features that we are interested in developing but are subject to implementation timeframe are ripple effects resulting from the interaction of sphere with water (moving around the sphere or applying the gravity effect) and real-time caustics from the light on the pool floor. The demo video would showcase both the static scene and dynamic interactions of the above features. Our assessment would be based on how good the quality of the ripple simulation is, how successful the interactive features work in real-time, and how fast the real-time rendering would react to each user interaction (f/s).
